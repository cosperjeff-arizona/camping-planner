Camping Trip: Route, Timing, & Campsite Selection

Route from Phoenix Area

Assuming you are starting from the Phoenix metro area, your route is straightforward:

  1. Take I-17 North towards Flagstaff.
  2. Travel for approximately 130-140 miles, depending on your starting point.
  3. Take Exit 306 for Stoneman Lake Road.
  4. Turn right (east) onto Stoneman Lake Rd, which is also designated as Forest Road (FS) 213.
  5. Follow FS 213 for a few miles. You will pass the main turnoff for the Stoneman Lake viewpoint.
  6. According to your map, you will eventually make a right turn onto FS 230. Follow this until you see the turn for FS 230E on your left.
  7. The area you've marked with a red arrow on your map is along this road.

Timing Analysis

Your timeline is tight but very doable if you are efficient.

  • 10:00 AM: Arrive home from T-ball. Final packing of cooler and last-minute items.
  • 1:00 PM: Depart from Phoenix.
  • ~2:45 - 3:15 PM: Arrive at Exit 306 (Stoneman Lake Rd). This accounts for about 1.5-2 hours of driving.
  • ~3:15 - 4:00 PM: Drive the forest roads (FS 213 -> FS 230 -> FS 230E) to find the perfect spot. Speed on these roads is slow (15-25 mph).
  • Sunset on Nov 8, 2025 will be around 5:28 PM.

This gives you roughly 1.5 to 2 hours of solid daylight to select your site, set up your tent, get organized, and gather firewood before dark.

Campsite Selection on FS 230E

This is dispersed camping, which means no designated sites, toilets, or water.

  • Look for existing spots: Drive slowly down FS 230E and look for small pull-offs or clearings that already have a stone fire ring. Using an existing site minimizes your impact on the land.
  • Safety First: When you find a spot, look up! Avoid parking or setting up your tent directly under large, dead branches (often called "widowmakers").
  • Leave No Trace: Choose a spot on a durable surface (dirt, pine needles) at least 200 feet from any water sources and the main road.
