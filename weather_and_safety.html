Temperature Details

Expect pleasant daytime highs in the mid-50s to low 60s Fahrenheit (12-17¬∞ C). However, as soon as the sun goes down, temperatures will plummet. Nighttime lows can easily drop into the 20s (-6 to -1¬∞ C), and sometimes even the teens. Be prepared for freezing temperatures overnight.

Fire Restrictions

This is the most important thing to check. The fall can be very dry.

Action: A few days before you leave, you MUST check the official Coconino National Forest website or call the Flagstaff Ranger District (928-526-0866) for the latest fire restrictions. If restrictions are in place, you may only be able to use a propane stove and not have a wood campfire.

Firewood

Assuming campfires are allowed, you can gather dead and down wood. Do not cut down standing trees, living or dead. It's a good idea to bring a couple of starter logs or a bundle of firewood from a store just in case the wood you find is damp.

Water

There is no potable water. You must bring all the water you will need for drinking, cooking, and cleaning. A good rule of thumb is 1.5 gallons per person, per day.
